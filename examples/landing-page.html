Who we are?

One thing that we’ve been able to take away from our highschool experiences so far is that it feels like everything is more difficult than it should be. In particular, volunteering and obtaining information about service opportunities. We can personally attest to the fact that we’ve met so many students with big hearts, but with no means to use them. It’s safe to say that accessibility and ease is a large part of our everyday lives, especially with the way our world has evolved technologically.




"Using music and art, children are able to escape their realities for a couple of hours. Being able to take away the stress from these kids, even for just a moment, will positively impact them in the long run."







So how can Operation One Step address this problem?

Homeless shelter systems are intended to provide short-term emergency assistance. Today, the Atlanta shelter system is large, growing and not serving its intended purpose because of the absence of a good exit strategy from the system. It is ultimately the children that need the most care. Children raised in homelessness have reduced literacy and vocabularies. These children are also less likely to have well-developed motor skills, and nearly half are unable to pass state-mandated testing at their grade level. Chronic stress and anxiety caused by poverty have also been linked to lower academic performance. These academic difficulties lead to higher dropout rates, limiting future opportunities. This contributes to multi-generational cycles of poverty. To address this challenge, Operation One Step facilitates the connection between student volunteers who are willing to teach, mentor, and advise disadvantaged children living in shelters in order to eventually break the cycle and rewrite the story. We offer more than a Band-Aid approach to homelessness by transforming lives and opening doors to life opportunities.
